THE CHRONICLES OF PIXEL KNIGHT

In the beginning, there was the Grid.

Long ago, before the age of pixels, the world was formless and void. Then the Great Programmer spoke, and pixels were arranged into order. Mountains rose from brown 8x8 tiles. Oceans pooled in azure squares. And from the first render cycle, creatures emerged — both beautiful and terrible.

You are the Pixel Knight. Not just any knight — the last knight. The others were claimed by the shadows long ago. Knight #1 was eaten by a crocodile on day one. Knight #2 forgot to eat and slowly starved. Knight #3 was a vampire who accidentally stood in direct sunlight, which in retrospect was a real design oversight. Knights #4 through #423 are too depressing to discuss in detail.

Now it falls to you.

THE LORE OF THE PETS

Your companion joins you on this journey. They are loyal, occasionally competent, and sometimes remember to do their job.

The Dog has served the Knight since the beginning of the realm. It once saved seventeen lives in a single campaign and then immediately knocked over a ceremonial torch, setting the victory hall ablaze. A net positive, all things considered.

The Cat assists when it feels like it — which statistically is about 40% of the time. Among cats, this is considered exceptional dedication.

The Chicken is an unexpectedly devastating combat unit. The royal archives do not explain this. No one asks.

The Rabbit has survived longer than any other companion in recorded history through a combination of raw speed and a sheer refusal to die. Scholars are baffled.

The Snake is venomous and completely unbothered by social norms or battle etiquette.

The Bird patrols the skies and delivers destruction from above at intervals of its own choosing.

The Hamster stores emergency supplies in its cheeks. The supplies are gold coins. This has not been explained by any academic institution.

The Turtle is slow and indestructible. This is its entire personality and it is entirely sufficient.

THE ENEMIES

The creatures that hunt you are many. All of them want you specifically dead — not the NPCs, not the trees, not the livestock. You, personally.

Slimes are simple and tenacious, jiggling toward you with cheerful indifference to their own mortality.

Skeletons keep their distance and shoot bone arrows — they are cowards, which is tactically sound.

Wraiths are translucent, fast, and deeply unpleasant.

Trolls are enormous, powerful, ugly, and shrug off most knockback.

Imps are small and nearly twice as fast as you'd like them to be. some, when halved in health, they split. Do not be surprised.

Golems are slow walking fortresses — the kind of enemy that reminds you that speed is not a virtue.

Vampires have stolen the name of one of our playable characters. This is considered a form of identity theft. Although their lawyers are working on defending the case, it is a losing battle.

Spiders are creepy and drop webs that slow your movement.

Wizards blast you from a distance and retreat when you approach.

Demons charge and enrage when wounded. They are also kind of mean, and this is coming from a monster specialist.

Mimics sit still and pretend to be harmless until you get close. They are not harmless. Quite the opposite actually.

Necromancers raise undead reinforcements and will not stop until something stops them first.

The Grim Reaper is a last resort — the realm's final answer when all else has failed to end you. He arrives once you've encountered every enemy type and reached wave 120, and only when the situation is truly dire. He is not symbolic. He moves at 2.5 tiles per second, hurls scythes in every direction, drains your soul from across the field, and accelerates when injured. He can also teleport. There is no lore explanation for this. He just can.

THE CROCODILES — AND THEIR ENEMIES

The crocodiles were here before you. They will be here after. They live in the lakes and will not negotiate. Engaging with thirty of them as the Fat character and subsequently being killed by one of them unlocks a certain other character. The universe has a sense of humor.

The Lava Salamanders are the crocodiles' ancient rivals. They emerged from the magma pools long ago, and the two species have maintained a cold territorial feud ever since — cold being relative when one side is literally on fire. If a lake and a lava pool are close enough, their residents will stop ignoring each other and start attacking each other instead. Diplomatically, this is considered a setback. For you, it is an opportunity. Stand back.

Note: lava and water cannot physically touch. Where the two would meet, the land has simply become stone. No one agreed to this. It simply is.

Larger lakes — those vast enough to have their own ecosystem — may also host Sharks. They are not crocodiles. They are worse, in a slightly different way. A shark patrols the deep water near the lake it was born in and will return there if it strays too far. They are large, fast, and possess a quantity of HP that is not what you would call reassuring. The good news is they cannot follow you to shore. The bad news is you may need to enter the water first.

THE DEPTHS

Lakes are not merely puddles with ambition. Beneath the waterline, the terrain continues. Submerged stone passages connect lakes that appear wholly separate from the surface — visible only to those who dare swim through them. Most warriors never discover this. The Diver does. Almost no one else does.

Fish inhabit these waters peacefully, schooling in loose formations and minding their own business. Strike one and the nearby ones become somewhat less peaceful. They are not dangerous until provoked. Provocation is not recommended unless prepared.

Sharks are apex predators of the deeper lakes. They patrol their home territory with cold efficiency. If you are underwater and a large grey shape appears in your peripheral vision, that is your only warning. Act accordingly.

A note for those inclined to explore: certain treasures rest on the lakebed that land-bound warriors will never find. The tradeoff is sharing the water with the things described above. Weigh this accordingly.

THE FORESTS

The world's trees do not grow uniformly. They cluster — by soil, by climate, by ancient whim. Wander far enough and you will find forests: tight groves of a single type, sometimes stretching dozens of tiles in every direction.

Oak trees are the default. Common, honest, moderately flammable. Chop them for gold.

Pine trees grow near stone. They are sturdier than oak and drop Pine Wood when felled — a material with several uses, including modest torchlight and basic barricade construction.

Fruit trees grow near water. They are small, fast to chop, and heal you when destroyed. This is probably the closest thing in this realm to healthcare.

Dead trees are grey, bare, and contain exactly one bone. Nobody knows whose it is. Consider not thinking about it.

Redwood trees are enormous. They can only be found in dedicated Redwood Forests, which are rare. They take significant effort to fell and drop Redwood — a superior building material. A torch made from Redwood lasts sixty seconds. A barricade made from it holds substantially longer than one assembled from impulse and pine needles.

WOOD, TORCHES, AND BARRICADES

Two types of wood exist in this world. Both are earned by chopping trees. Both can be sold at the shop. Neither can be eaten, despite several attempts.

Press F to light a torch. This consumes one piece of wood and widens the light around you during night — critical, because the night in this realm is genuinely, profoundly dark. Redwood torches burn for a full minute. Pine torches burn for fifteen seconds and should be considered a last resort. The torch is held, not placed, eaten, thrown, used as a sword, or any other manner that is not holding it. It travels with you. It goes out on its own schedule, which it communicates via notification.

Press G to deploy a barricade at your current position. This costs five pieces of wood and produces a structure that blocks enemy movement until it is destroyed. Enemies will destroy it. The question is whether they do so before or after you've created enough distance to matter.

Wood can also be exchanged at the shop for gold, or used to apply a Timber Upgrade to your active weapon, granting it knockback on every hit. Why this is helpful has not been explained, but it just is.

THE WEAPONS

You begin with a sword — reliable, honest, and incapable of embarrassment. Through commerce, fortune, and perseverance, you may eventually wield flame, ice, lightning, and things that should not technically exist. The Blacksmith repairs and enhances your arsenal. Prices are non-negotiable and complaints are not accepted.

The shop now allows you to lock an item between waves — preventing it from being replaced on rerolls or auto-refreshes. Locked items accrue a 1% price increase per wave. This is called market dynamics and it is your fault for not buying sooner.

HARD - BUT HARDER

There is a rumor — repeated in hushed, slightly embarrassed tones across the realm — that the difficulty settings are not the final word. That somewhere beyond the hardest officially acknowledged difficulty, there exists another. The realm does not confirm or deny this. The realm only says: it exists for those who have already proven something, and it is very much worse than what came before.

THE CYCLE OF DAY AND NIGHT

Day is relatively safe. Night brings faster, more aggressive enemies with heightened senses. The transition is announced with adequate warning. There is no excuse for being caught unprepared.

At full night, the world becomes pitch black. A faint glow surrounds you — enough to see immediately ahead. If you have wood, light a torch. If you do not have wood, the consequences are yours to discover.

THE GOLD

Forty percent of all gold earned in a run enters the lifelong treasury upon your death. What this treasury funds is not fully documented. The economy of the pixel realm operates on principles not yet understood by mortal accounting.

A FINAL NOTE

If you have read all of this: you are a rare and patient warrior. Proceed into battle with our blessing.

If you have not: we understand. You are busy. The adventure simply begins.

Good luck, Pixel Knight. You'll need it.

THE WIZARD'S TRIAL

Runes replace weapons. Mana charges over time — when full, press a rune slot key to cast. Repair runes at the Blacksmith. Survive to the target wave to unlock the Wizard.

HOW TO PLAY

CONTROLS
WASD — Move  |  Click — Attack  |  Space — Dash
1–5 — Switch weapon  |  P — Shop  |  B — Blacksmith  |  ESC — Pause
F — Light torch (uses wood; expands your light during night)
G — Deploy barricade (5 Pine or 2 Redwood; blocks enemies)
X — Craft Skeleton Warrior (5 Bones; fights by your side)
BASIC SURVIVAL
Kill enemies to earn gold and score. Every 1500 score advances the wave.
Waves 10, 20, 30… are boss waves. Waves 5, 15, 25… are horde waves.
Enemies get faster and tougher each wave. The Grim Reaper arrives at wave 120+.
TREES & WOOD
Oak — drops gold when chopped.
Pine — drops Pine Wood. Use for torches (15s) or barricades (5 needed).
Fruit — heals 5 HP when chopped.
Dead — drops a Bone. 5 Bones → craft a Skeleton Warrior (X key).
Redwood — rare, in forests only. Drops Redwood; torch lasts 60s. Sell high.
NIGHT
At night the world goes pitch black. You have a small light around you. Light a torch with F to extend your vision.
Night enemies are faster and more aggressive. Be prepared.
SHOP & BLACKSMITH
Buy weapons in the Shop. Lock a slot so it survives rerolls — but the price rises 1% per wave (max +150%).
The Blacksmith repairs durability, upgrades weapons, and crafts fusion weapons.
WORLD HAZARDS
Water — Crocodiles live here. They're territorial and aggressive.
Lava — Salamanders live here. They deal fire damage and fight crocs if their pools border.
Standing in lava or water slows you and deals damage over time.
